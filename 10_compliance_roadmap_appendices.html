17 Compliance Considerations

17.1 Software Licensing

  • cert-manager: Apache 2.0 licence (FOSS-compliant)

  • HashiCorp Vault: Not managed by this operating model. If adopted by Cequence Ops or OKAPI Ops, licence terms vary by edition; confirm with Procurement/Legal.

  • AppViewX (DCERTS): Commercial licence (managed by PKI team)

  • OpenSSL: Apache 2.0 licence for v3.x

17.2 Export Control

Private key operations and cryptographic tooling distribution may trigger export controls:

  • Australian-hosted key operations: Perform key generation, storage, and all private key operations (e.g., TLS handshakes) only in Australian-resident environments (cloud AU regions or Australian on-premises). Confirm compliance by identifying and evidencing all termination points for each traffic path before making non-compliance determinations.

  • Termination mapping: For each customer-facing endpoint_fqdn, document where TLS terminates for every connection leg: (a) consumer \(\rightarrow\) defender, (b) defender \(\rightarrow\) upstream (SaaS or multi-cloud origin), and (c) any intermediate hops performing TLS termination (CDN/WAF, LB, reverse proxy, service mesh, managed gateway). The evidence pack must include region/site identifiers and the terminating component identity. Include a reproducible handshake test showing the served certificate chain and fingerprint (see Appendix 22).

  • Roadmap compatibility: The multi-cloud roadmap (AWS \(\rightarrow\) Azure \(\rightarrow\) on-prem) is compatible with this constraint only when each phase is AU-hosted with supporting evidence (region/site, tenancy, replication/backups/log sinks, and DR design). Where an upstream SaaS terminates TLS outside Australia, or the termination location cannot be evidenced, treat it as an open constraint. Resolve it only after Legal/Compliance and architecture define the acceptable pattern.

  • No cross-cloud private key movement: Do not export or transfer production private key material between clouds/tenancies/regions. Cross-cloud migration requires generating a new key in the target environment and re-issuing the certificate (CSR submitted to DCERTS), not moving an existing private key.

  • Classify automation artefacts under ECCN 5D002 or EAR99 as appropriate

  • Vendor support access to key material may breach contractual controls

17.3 Data Residency

  • Production private keys: Australian-resident HSM/KMS/Vault (no cross-region replication; backups and audit logs retained in Australia).

  • Roadmap evidence requirement: For each secrets-platform phase (AWS EKS, Azure, on-prem), explicitly record the hosting location (region/site) and demonstrate it is AU-resident before storing/operating on production private keys.

  • Evidence packs: Controlled repository with retention and legal hold support

  • EAB and TSIG secrets: Australian-resident secrets platform

19 Conclusion

Certificate lifecycle management in split custody environments demands systematic engineering. Manual workflows create operational risk through coordination gaps, validation inconsistencies, and fragmented secrets management.

Five findings emerge from this analysis:

  1. The DCERTS ACME integration is blocked by a server-side error requiring vendor resolution

  2. Split-custody coordination between Cequence and OKAPI teams lacks automation

  3. Control gates for CSR validation, chain building, and deployment verification are manual or absent

  4. Secrets are distributed across multiple stores without unified governance

  5. Non-Telstra CA onboarding and custom domain enablement lack formal controls; this analysis defines a trust anchor allowlist, domain authorisation lifecycle, tiered governance framework, and Operational Readiness Review (ORR) gate to close these gaps

The recommended path forward prioritises:

  1. Establishing a certificate inventory and transitioning it to an authoritative source of truth once integrity controls are operating

  2. Implementing deterministic validation gates

  3. Pre-approving emergency change templates

  4. Resolving DCERTS ACME blocker to enable automation

  5. Confirming secrets management custody boundaries with vendor and OKAPI teams

  6. Deploying the ORR gate and trust anchor allowlist to enable non-Telstra CA onboarding (R6), starting with ACM delegation for vendor-managed certificates

  7. Operationalising domain authorisation for custom domain routing (R7), phased from Telstra-owned domains to BYO domains

This roadmap moves the operating model toward higher automation and auditability, within split custody and DCERTS constraints. Telecommunications regulatory requirements apply when validated by Legal/Compliance and backed by a service-specific evidence pack.
